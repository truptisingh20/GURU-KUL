What is Classical Mechanics?

Classical mechanics is the branch of physics that deals with the motion of objects and the forces that act upon them. It provides the foundation for understanding phenomena ranging from planetary motion to everyday mechanics.

1. Newton's Laws of Motion

Newton's laws are the cornerstone of classical mechanics. These laws describe the relationship between a body and the forces acting upon it, laying the groundwork for analyzing motion.

2. Work and Energy

Work is the transfer of energy through force, and energy comes in various forms, including kinetic and potential. The conservation of energy is a fundamental principle in physics.

3. Rotational Dynamics

Rotational dynamics examines the motion of objects that rotate about an axis, involving concepts such as torque, angular momentum, and moment of inertia.

4. Gravitation

Gravitation is the force of attraction between objects with mass. Newton's law of universal gravitation explains the motion of celestial bodies and tides on Earth.

5. Harmonic Motion

Simple harmonic motion describes systems like springs and pendulums, where the restoring force is proportional to the displacement from equilibrium.

6. Fluid Mechanics

Fluid mechanics studies the behavior of liquids and gases, including concepts like buoyancy, pressure, and viscosity.
