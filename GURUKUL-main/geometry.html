Introduction

Geometry is a branch of mathematics that studies the properties and relationships of points, lines, surfaces, and solids. It is one of the oldest mathematical disciplines, essential in fields like art, architecture, and engineering.

1. Geometric Shapes

Geometry explores a variety of shapes, including:
- **2D Shapes**: Circles, triangles, rectangles, squares.
- **3D Shapes**: Cubes, spheres, cones, cylinders.
Each shape has unique properties such as area, perimeter, and volume.

2. Key Formulas

Geometry involves several essential formulas for calculations:
- **Perimeter and Area**: For 2D shapes like rectangles and circles.
- **Volume and Surface Area**: For 3D shapes like spheres and cubes.
- **The Pythagorean Theorem**: \(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\), used in right triangles.

3. Applications of Geometry

Geometry has practical applications in many areas, including:
- **Architecture and Design**: Creating blueprints and 3D models.
- **Engineering**: Analyzing structures and mechanical systems.
- **Art**: Developing perspective and proportions.
- **Geography**: Understanding maps and spatial relationships.
