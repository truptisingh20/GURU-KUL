What is Particle Physics?

Particle physics is the branch of physics that studies the fundamental particles of the universe and the forces by which they interact. It seeks to understand the very fabric of matter and energy at the most fundamental level.

1. Fundamental Particles

Particles like quarks, leptons, and bosons form the building blocks of matter. The Standard Model describes these particles and their interactions.

2. The Four Fundamental Forces

Particle physics explores the four forces: gravitational, electromagnetic, weak nuclear, and strong nuclear, which govern the interactions of particles.

3. Experiments in Particle Physics

State-of-the-art experiments like those conducted at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) help scientists discover new particles and test theories like supersymmetry and string theory.

4. Applications

Advances in particle physics have led to innovations like medical imaging (PET scans), particle accelerators, and improvements in computing and materials science.
