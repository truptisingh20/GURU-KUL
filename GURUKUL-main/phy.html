Introduction

Physical Chemistry bridges the gap between chemistry and physics, studying how matter behaves on a molecular and atomic level. This field is crucial in understanding chemical reactions, energy changes, and the behavior of materials. Key concepts include thermodynamics, kinetics, quantum mechanics, and more.

1. Thermodynamics

Laws of Thermodynamics First Law: Energy can neither be created nor destroyed, only converted from one form to another. Second Law: The entropy (degree of disorder) of an isolated system always increases over time. Third Law: The entropy of a perfect crystal approaches zero as temperature approaches absolute zero. Applications: Understanding heat, work, and energy transfer in chemical reactions. Key principles in calculating the energy changes during reactions, such as enthalpy (ŒîH), entropy (ŒîS), and Gibbs free energy (ŒîG).

4. Electrochemistry

Electrochemistry deals with the interconversion of chemical and electrical energy. It explains processes like oxidation and reduction, and is applied in electrochemical cells and batteries.

5. Quantum Chemistry

Quantum Chemistry explores the behavior of matter and energy at atomic and subatomic levels using principles of quantum mechanics. This branch explains molecular structure and reaction mechanisms at a deep level.
