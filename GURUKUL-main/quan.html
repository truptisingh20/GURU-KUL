What is Quantum Mechanics?

Quantum Mechanics is a fundamental branch of physics that describes the behavior of matter and energy on the smallest scales. It challenges classical concepts, introducing principles like wave-particle duality and quantum superposition.

1. Wave-Particle Duality

Particles such as electrons exhibit both particle-like and wave-like behavior, depending on how they are observed. This duality forms the cornerstone of quantum mechanics.

2. Quantum Superposition

Quantum systems can exist in multiple states simultaneously until they are measured, collapsing into a single state. This concept powers quantum computing.

3. Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

This principle states that certain properties of a particle, such as position and momentum, cannot be simultaneously measured with arbitrary precision.

4. Quantum Entanglement

Entangled particles remain connected, such that the state of one instantly influences the state of the other, no matter how far apart they are.

5. Applications

Quantum Mechanics underpins technologies like semiconductors, lasers, quantum computing, and even medical imaging techniques.
