What is Relativity?

Relativity is a branch of physics developed by Albert Einstein, comprising the theories of Special and General Relativity. It provides a framework for understanding the relationship between space, time, and gravity, fundamentally altering classical mechanics.

1. Special Relativity

Special Relativity focuses on objects moving at constant speeds, introducing concepts like time dilation, length contraction, and the equivalence of mass and energy through the famous equation \(E = mc^2\).

2. General Relativity

General Relativity extends the principles of Special Relativity to include gravity, describing it as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy. It predicts phenomena like black holes and gravitational waves.

3. Key Predictions

Relativity predicts astonishing phenomena, including time dilation near massive objects, the bending of light around stars, and the expansion of the universe.

4. Applications

The principles of relativity are essential in technologies like GPS, where precise time measurements are corrected for relativistic effects, and in understanding the universe's origin and evolution.
