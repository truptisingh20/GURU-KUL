Introduction

Statistics is a branch of mathematics that involves collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data. It is used to understand trends, make predictions, and guide decision-making in various fields such as economics, business, social sciences, and more.

1. Measures of Central Tendency

Measures of central tendency are used to summarize a set of data by identifying the central position within that set. The most common measures are:

  • Mean: The arithmetic average of a set of numbers.
  • Median: The middle value when the data is arranged in order.
  • Mode: The value that appears most frequently in a data set.

Example: Find the mean, median, and mode of the data set: 3, 7, 7, 2, 9, 8, 10.

2. Measures of Dispersion

Measures of dispersion describe the spread or variability of data. Common measures of dispersion include:

  • Range: The difference between the maximum and minimum values in the data set.
  • Variance: The average of the squared differences from the mean.
  • Standard Deviation: The square root of the variance, representing the average distance from the mean.

Example: Find the range, variance, and standard deviation of the data set: 5, 7, 8, 12, 14.

3. Real-Life Applications

>

Statistics plays a key role in a wide range of fields, from medical research and economics to sports and politics. It helps to make informed decisions, understand trends, and forecast future events based on data.
