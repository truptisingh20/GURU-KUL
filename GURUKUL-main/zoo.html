Discover the World of Animals

Zoology is the branch of biology that studies the animal kingdom, including their structure, evolution, and behavior.

What is Zoology?

Zoology is a scientific discipline that focuses on the study of animals, their anatomy, physiology, classification, behavior, and ecological roles. It covers a wide range of topics, from microscopic organisms to large mammals.

1. Animal Anatomy and Physiology

Understanding the internal and external structures of animals and how they function is a core aspect of zoology.

2. Animal Behavior

Zoology examines how animals interact with their environment, including mating, hunting, migration, and communication behaviors.

3. Classification of Animals

Animals are categorized into groups such as mammals, reptiles, amphibians, birds, and invertebrates based on shared characteristics.

4. Evolution and Adaptation

Zoology explores the evolutionary history of animals and how they have adapted to survive in different environments.

5. Ecology and Conservation

The role of animals in ecosystems and efforts to protect endangered species are critical areas of zoological research.

6. Marine Biology

Marine biology is a subfield of zoology that studies the diverse life forms found in oceans and seas.
